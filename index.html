Glaucoma Introduction

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye diseases that can cause vision loss and blindness by damaging a nerve in the back of your eye called the optic nerve. Glaucoma includes: normal tension Glaucoma: primary open-angle Glaucoma, angle-closure Glaucoma and other types. It impacts millions of people in the world

Recognizing Glaucoma

There are very few early warning signs for glaucoma. Some people may have warning signs but most patients will not notice any vision loss until the disease is at a late stage. The best way to know if you have it is to get regular eye exams. Common signs of glaucoma include: severe eye pain, nausea, redness in the eye, blurred vision, and seeing colored rings around lights.

Living with Glaucoma

Managing glaucoma and enjoying a better life with glaucoma involves medications regular eye check-ups, avoiding accidents, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle. For example, a healthy lifestyle that includes regular exercise and a nutritious diet is especially important. In addition, Maintaining mental and emotional health is also important.
